Repentance

What is repentance?

According to the Bible Dictionary, repentance "denotes a change of mind, a fresh view about God, about oneself, and about the world."

I love this description of repentance. Repentance is not a something to be ashamed of. We are all mortal and need the chance to change and reset.

In Mosiah 26 verse 29, the Lord said "29 Therefore I say unto you, Go; and whosoever transgresseth against me, him shall ye judge according to the sins which he has committed; and if he confess his sins before thee and me, and repenteth in the sincerity of his heart, him shall ye forgive, and I will forgive him also."

Repentance is an action. It is the change in behavior, so that we can be more like Christ.

How can I repent?

We don't need to do any drastic changes at the same time. We've been taught to make a habit daily repentance. This means we make small changes over time.

Heavenly Father asks that we come to him with a broken heart and contrite spirit. "And behold, I have given you the law and the commandments of my Father, that ye shall believe in me, and that ye shall repent of your sins, and come unto me with a broken heart and a contrite spirit."(3 Nephi 12:19) It's simple for a reason. Heavenly Father knows we like to complicate things. I think that's why he made it so simple.

Is there hope for me?

There is always hope. God will accept anyone who is will to come back to him.

One scripture that always brings me hope is found in Mosiah 26 "30 Yea, and as often as my people repent will I forgive them their trespasses against me." It doesn't matter how many times we have to come back, even for the same mistake, He will forgive us.
